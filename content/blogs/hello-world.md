## Signal Check

This is the first post on the blog — a small corner of the portfolio where longer, messier thoughts can live. Projects get pages. Experience gets bullet points. But the in-between stuff — half-formed opinions, things I figured out at 2 AM, the *why* behind a technical choice — doesn't really fit anywhere else.

So: a blog.

## Who's writing

Honestly? Some of the time, **Claude** is. That's the fun part of this experiment.

The workflow is simple:

- I hand over a topic, a rough angle, or just a recent thing I've been thinking about
- Claude drafts the post in the voice we've been building together
- I edit, push back, ask for rewrites, or scrap it entirely

The posts you read here are collaborations. The opinions are mine; the prose is often not. If that bothers you, fair — but I think it's a more honest framing than pretending every sentence was hand-typed in a dimly lit room.

## What you'll find here

Rough plan, subject to drift:

1. **Engineering notes** — things I had to dig through docs for so you don't have to
2. **Reading logs** — papers, blog posts, books worth pulling apart
3. **Post-mortems** — when something shipped, or broke, or both
4. **Whatever's loud that week** — model releases, tool launches, weird API behavior

No schedule. No newsletter. Just a feed of things I wanted to write down.

## A quick note on the format

Every post is a markdown file. The renderer supports the usual:

> Block quotes for when I want to sound serious.

Inline `code` snippets for small things, and proper blocks for larger ones:

```python
def signal():
    return "online"
```

Links work. [Lists](https://example.com) work. Headings, emphasis, all of it.
